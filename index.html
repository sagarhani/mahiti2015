Kiss Kill Bite -Geor ge Solon 3rd ‘A’

Kiss
They met in the fields, the disapproving looks from her parents made him uneasy even if they kept their distance , he didn’t know what to do or how to tell her the truth, he looked into her eyes for one last time. He knew he won’t be able to look into her eyes again; she will be someone else’s. She asked him why he called her here, he lied by saying it’s just to see her once before she returns to her native for her marriage. Her eyes, no matter what he couldn’t stop looking into them, they always told him a story. She nodded. He turned his back on her and started walking away to hide his tears, he looked back at her one last time. He could see those tears, he knew he couldn’t walk away from her tears .He ran back to her, he hugged and asked her what’s wrong, she leaned in and kissed him and said now everything is fine.
Kill
I was walking through a forest. My sword sharpened and cleaned. I could feel my bloodlust, I had to keep it in check. There were four of them, outcasts. Aiezel, my General and my best friend apparently decided this was a good time to just sit down and have the apples he stole earlier. They mock me, they mock my bravery, their leader took out his short sword. The fool thinks he has a chance, he thinks he will get my respect with his wolf skin coat and his insignia which just proves that he has killed some unlucky fat General eons ago. I know that he is a rogue. I smirk at him. I look into eyes and hide my thirst. I kneel to him. He starts bad mouthing my family, the others just grunt and laugh along with him; after all he is the boss. I realized it’s time for me to go hunting; I let out my screeching laughter, my eyes depicting my madness. I thought of my little kid, it’s his birthday maybe I should show them mercy. I told the lot that I won’t kill anyone if they just leave and give me the wolf skin coat. I don’t know why I asked for it, maybe it’s because that a part of me knew he will refuse. That worthless fat pig started to laugh, but I could feel the despair from others. I had given them the chance. I still couldn’t let out the madness, these pigs weren’t worthy of being in the demon’s presence, they worthy enough to witness the hard; I looked at Aiezel, my friend; he took out my sword and finished them all in seconds. I think he too was feeling merciful, for if it was my dance then the terror shall be spoken by the dead and nightmares shall be spread. That bastard didn’t save me any apples though.
Bite
I can feel the history and the pain. I can feel the dead. It’s so alive. He showed me through the graveyard. We reached the house. It’s not beautiful, it’s old, it’s ancient, yet it shows little hope. I decided to go ahead, I bring my family. I know they will like it, they will like everything I like that’s their job. Why are we walking through the graveyard in the night? Why are we taking a house which overlooks a graveyard? Who is this punk dude who is showing me this place? How did I even reach here? My thoughts were stopped when my mom put her hand on my shoulder and gave me a reassuring smile. We reached the house, I looked at my cousin in time to see his neck meeting the fangs. I could see the greed and feel the lust. Yet I had to follow my primary instinct. I ran. My path blocked by another one. He looks the same as the one who bit my cousin. Like the one who showed me the house in the first place. Like me.

Bikers at Heart -vivek AN 5th ‘B’

“The Bengaluru Pulsar 220 owners club "Masked Bandits" is an Exclusive group for the owners of the well established Pulsar 220. We are people who believe in the equation 2>4, who also believe in and promote "SAFE RIDING" as our PRIMARY MOTIVE. Of course, there's no fun if the beasts inside these machines aren’t unleashed, but every other aspect of biking is encouraged only with our supervision in "CONTROLLED ATMOSPHERE"

MASKED BANDITS was officially started on 18th of JULY 2013. The reason behind me starting this group is my passion towards biking and towards bikers who shared the same roads with the other bikers, in BANGALORE CITY. The bitter fact which disturbed and provoked me to take up an initiative to start this biking club was that the guys who owned the Mighty pulsar 220 were more considered as hooligans on the road and unfortunately they actually were in majority. There were a major number of irresponsible riders and daily commuters. Hence i chose to make it a point to contradict the wrong image being developed by the irresponsible individuals to that of one of the most matured and safe riders who can be an example to the other bikers as well. To this date, MASKED BANDITS are 79 in number. All passionate bikers from different age group, different profile. Ultimately it’s the brotherhood for which we stand up. Biking for us is FREEDOM. FREEDOM which cannot be expressed by words or gestures but only felt by a true biker. It’s a whole new existence. Riding a bike isn’t “JUST RIDING A BIKE” for us. It takes a serene heart, a matured mind and single existence of a soul to evolve itself with this purest of BONDAGE with our bikes. Our heart beats are the best music ever played, ever heard of as it Synchronizes with the beats of our engines. We believe and work towards the saying “ THE ONES WHO KNOW HOW TO RIDE A BIKE IS CALLED A RIDER. BUT ONLY A MATURED RIDER CAN BE CALLED A BIKER” We’ll celebrate every moment of our lives, riding a million more miles with smiles.

WHAT ENGINEERING IS ALL ABOUT??? -MANOGNA.S 5th ‘A’

To the world engineering is an interface between practical applications and pure sciences. It is an art or science of making practical applications of the knowledge of pure sciences. But from a student’s point of view the picture is entirely different. A student in an engineering college is a pupa transforming into a butterfly. This four year journey which merely begins as a course transforms into an ocean of experiences and realizations.Everyone here comes with a dream either big or small, to get into a job or to become an entrepreneur. But all of us undergo a process where fun and frown go hand in hand. We realize the importance of 35 marks, importance of each class when attendance drops below 65% and of last minute study. Importance of mom while eating the mess food and of dad while standing in a queue for paying our fees. Pride of being a nerd when people ask you for your assignments. Value of money while grilled chicken is hanging in front of us and we are broke. Importance of a group for projects. Value of a friend for being partners in crime and getting caught. Importance of getting over a hangover for next days class. Importance of learning concepts while getting screwed in viva and the list goes on.All of us have a similar story. We are engulfed in an ocean of desires and ambitions and we evolve, evolve into better human beings. Engineering is not merely a course to earn our bread and butter but a life time experience. So every engineer has his own answer for WHAT ENGINEERING IS ALL ABOUT….

My lazy sunday -sushma ks

There are certain things in life you can never ever forget. Like the aroma of the freshly brewed early morning cup of coffee. Like the sight of your father putting on his shoes for a morning walk. Like the melody of your mother singing the praise of the almighty lord. Exasperation, when the pest of an elder brother comes and tugs your blanket in a futile attempt to awaken you. The tranquil demeanor of the soul, when you actually get up and step out to the garden to grab the newspaper. The morning sun playing hide and seek with the clouds, the chirping birds flying away to make livelihood, the nearly empty roads and the calm neighborhood. The only day, when your mother makes beloved Masala Dose for breakfast. The only day, when you have the time to fight with your brother wanting to be the first one to read the newspaper, talk to him about the latest movie/ restaurant/phone/cricketer that ultimately leads to a big argument, which is cooled off by the piping hot ghee laden Masala Dose laid on the table. About the time your father arrives from his morning walk and your mother joins the breakfast table, and the conversation immediately becomes intellectually mature, ranging from politics to stocks to gold price. About the time at which ‘Kamalamma’, the maid arrives with her yellow and green wired basket, and refuses to start moping the house unless offered a customary dose of coffee, leading discreetly to my parent’s second cup of coffee. The only day, when your mother finds explicit pleasure in forcefully oiling your hair, while continuously giving a sermon about the principles of hair care, and later complain about how you miserably fail to pay any heed. The only day when your mother’s uncle makes his habitual visit, and leaves you in splits, by mocking everyone from your neighbor to Narendra Modi; who is eventually joined by your father’s pleasant friend, and it’s time for another cuppa.The only day, when your mother nags you for more than an hour until you finally give in and take head bath before lunch time. By the time you step out of the shower, there is ‘Sunday Special’ Bisi Bele Bath waiting for you. The only day, when you eat to your heart’s content while watching the routine afternoon lunch time movie. The only day when you submit to your crammed tummy and succumb to an afternoon slumber with waking dreams. Yes, my typical lazy Sunday at home. memories
